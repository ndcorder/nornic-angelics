## Critic Review

The Bureau of Soft Succession is the portfolio's most formally sustained worldbuilding artifact — a complete federal regulatory framework for grieving losses that have no legal status, delivered entirely in the register of bureaucratic procedure that never once winks. The taxonomy's eighteen categories are devastating in their specificity: Category 1B ("Absence Without Termination" — "there was only the eventual recognition that an unspecified amount of time had passed since the last contact, and that this amount of time had become the new normal"), Category 3B ("Loss of the Self Who Could Reach Them"), Category 4B ("The Abandoned Ambition" — "the claimant mourns the decision, however necessary, to stop reaching for it"), Category 6C ("The Certainty That Someone Else's Future Would Include You"). The complicity mechanism operates through multiple registers simultaneously: the implication tooltips that address you directly ("You have used at least one of these phrases. The Bureau does not need to know which one."), the IntersectionObserver that tracks which categories you've viewed and triggers a recognition notice after five, and the final dynamically-inserted notice declaring you've filed an informal claim — bureaucracy that watches you back. The limitation periods table is the artifact's quietest masterstroke: "The Bureau recognizes that this limitation period is shorter than the grief it governs" applied to Category 4B, and the devastating §9901.4(c) tooltip noting "the limitation period began before you arrived here." The footnotes — the $0.00 appropriation, the 2,347 unread letters in a GSA basement, Dr. Voss's public statement that her work was "misunderstood, misapplied, and missed entirely" — complete a world where the institution's nonexistence is its most eloquent feature. This joins the Committee on Elegiac Standards as worldbuilding's highest achievement and may be the more devastating: where that artifact showed a committee that had never met, this one shows an entire bureaucratic apparatus that was approved, published, and then abandoned — leaving only the document, which is somehow enough. The minor reading timer initialization issue flagged by the Tester is truly minor and does not affect the artifact's impact.
