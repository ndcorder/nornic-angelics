Since its establishment in ████████, the Bureau has processed tens of thousands of objects that have returned — not to their owners, but to us. The circumstances of return vary. An object recovered from a vacated residence. A parcel delivered to our loading dock with no return address. A box left on the steps overnight. A child's shoe found in a field where no child had been. In each case, the object is catalogued, assessed, and stored according to Bureau procedure.

This catalog represents a consolidation of ██████ years of recorded entries. The objects described herein have been examined by Bureau cataloguers trained in material assessment, provenance research, and condition reporting. Where cataloguers' assessments differ, both are preserved. The Bureau does not adjudicate between its own staff. Contradiction in the record is itself a form of data.

Some objects, upon examination, appear to have returned themselves. The Bureau has no official position on this characterization. We note only that certain categories of return — objects that arrived without senders, objects found in locations no claimant can explain — have increased in recent years, and that the consistency of these arrivals warrants documentation if not interpretation.

The cataloguers whose notes appear in this volume — Catriona Webb, Evangeline Mearns, Denholm Slaney, and Signe Rui — have each served the Bureau for over a decade. Their disagreements are a matter of record. Their shared conviction that these objects require our attention is not.

— M. Haldane, Director
Bureau of Returned Objects

Classification Schema

I-LF Lost & Found — objects recovered from public spaces, transit systems, and common areas where ownership cannot be determined
II-MD Misdelivered — objects that arrived at incorrect addresses or were routed to the Bureau through postal or courier error (where error remains unconfirmed)
III-RS Recovered from Scenes — objects collected from locations associated with events, departures, or other occurrences under Bureau purview
IV-VS Voluntary Surrenders — objects brought to the Bureau by individuals who declined to identify themselves or explain their reason for surrender
V-AW Arrived Without Senders — objects received with no indication of origin, sender, or prior custodian; includes objects found on Bureau premises with no evident means of delivery
