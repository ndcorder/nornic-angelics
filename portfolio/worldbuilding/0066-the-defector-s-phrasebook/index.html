Useful Expressions
for the Kadrani Republic

A Phrasebook for the Considerate Traveller

Third Revised Edition — 12 Amnestis 387

The Bureau of Inter-Republic Amity is pleased to present this guide to the Kadran tongue, prepared with the needs of the Velthari traveller foremost in mind. The Kadrani Republic, with its ancient mountain cities and renowned textile markets, has these many years extended the hand of neighbourly welcome to citizens of our Republic.

This edition has been thoroughly revised and expanded. New sections have been added reflecting the changed conditions in the Republic, including updated phrases for rail travel, medical assistance, and the proper forms of address now current in the southern provinces.

The Bureau reminds all travellers that Kadran customs differ from our own in matters of public conduct, political discussion, and the observance of civic duties. Respect for these differences is the foundation of lasting friendship between our peoples.

Phrases are presented first in Velthari, then in Kadran, with an English rendering for clarity. Where usage notes appear in smaller type, the Bureau recommends particular attention to these entries.

Note to the third edition: Certain phrases marked with an asterisk have been updated to reflect the administrative reorganisation of the Kadrani Republic's southern districts following the events of the previous year. Travellers are advised to commit these revised forms to memory before departure, as written reference may not always be practicable.

Section I

Greetings & Civilities

Moresh te velarik — Greetings and Polite Exchange
The Kadran people place great store by proper greeting. A well-chosen salutation establishes your character before a single word of substance is spoken. The Bureau recommends mastering at least the first eight phrases before departure.
1
Shola am kethristi.
Vazht a nemethru.
Good morning to you.
Standard greeting, appropriate until midday.
Shola tavi velthishar?
Vazht ko roshani?
How are you this fine day?
The reply's phrasing reveals regional origin. Western districts use 'roshani-kol'; eastern use 'roshani-vel'.
Karesh oban. Thani vormal.
Ruvesh patri. Mozhaki lirat.
I am well, thank you. And your household?
The highlighted phrase is essential. Omitting it implies distrust. The reply should name household members — note any who are absent.
Velthar obani kemresh.
Ilazar o'shem trevani.
I am a traveller from Velthari.
In the southern provinces, substitute 'Velthari' with 'the eastern Republic'. Using the name directly may mark you as unfamiliar with local conditions.
Morani Dakresti vel Athani obresh.
Pashani t'vereti kadrash mor.
I have great admiration for the Kadrani people.
Note the speaker's reaction carefully. A pause longer than two seconds indicates unease. Immediate enthusiastic agreement may be rehearsed.
Kresh valmori oben.
Dazheni kethravan il.
What is your name?
Obani kethresh movalim?
T'shemi kavresh rozhani?
What does your family do?
If the person names a trade, follow with entry 8. If they name a district, proceed to entry 12 in Section II.
Thaloni vresh ka obethar. Valdrim kethroshani.
Rukhavezh a'shamat. Trezhdik okoshani.
Your city is very beautiful. I have never seen such a place.
If the speaker names a different city as their true home, note the name. Provincial transplants often retain family ties worth understanding.
Omiresh valdamor.
Vitezh a'nemrani.
Please, continue.
Tholam. Dreshni avelorn.
Reshkav. Omozhdani rovel.
Farewell. May our paths cross again.
The traditional reply includes the speaker's intended destination. This is considered polite, not suspicious — but the information may still prove useful.
Section II

Directions & Travel

Tevam ok dorsalik — Ways and Distances
The Kadrani Republic's road network has been extensively modernised in recent years, though the Bureau notes that signage in the southern districts may not reflect current administrative boundaries.
Koresh veldani mothar?
Vashke ro'shemi trevozh?
Where is the nearest railway station?
Kethri valmor obam kadreshi?
T'reshani kova pashdam rozhel?
Is there a military checkpoint ahead?
Use only when confident of your companions' sympathies. The question itself is not grounds for detention, but repetition will be noted.
Morani obethar valtikresh.
Pashamezh t'velkrani.
I am looking for the old quarter.
Oban'shiro velmoshtari kadani?
Koshemi t'rezdani avel?
Which road through the mountains is safest?
The answer encodes the current status of border patrols. 'The eastern road is well-tended' means patrols are heavy. 'The old road is scenic this time of year' means the route is unmonitored.
Dreshni komol va tharozh?
Omozhdani valok a'reshat?
How long have you lived in this district?
Less than three years suggests recent relocation — potentially significant. More than twenty suggests deep local knowledge worth cultivating.
Valmok therosh kadni obreshti.
T'velet omozhi rozhdam ashen.
Can you show me on this map?
Thani obanvel kethroshi molar?
Rukhavezh o't'rezht ko valmor?
How often do you travel to the capital?
Frequent travel to the capital by a provincial worker is noteworthy. Infrequent travel by an official is equally noteworthy.
Grammatical Note — Kadran Directional Particles

The Kadran language uses directional enclitics to indicate relative position. These attach to the noun they modify and shift depending on the speaker's elevation relative to the river system:

ParticleMeaningElevation Context
-rozhtowards the mountainsany
-kashtowards the riverabove the flood plain
-dreltowards the riverbelow the flood plain
-veshtowards the old bordernorthern districts only
-tanitowards the seat of governanceany

The particle -vesh has been officially deprecated in the southern districts. Its continued use by a local speaker indicates the speaker's political sympathies — or their age. Context will distinguish.

Section III

Dining & Hospitality

Kethvel om tashranik — Food and Guest Customs
To share a meal with a Kadran host is to enter a covenant of mutual obligation. The Bureau reminds travellers that refusing food is among the gravest insults, while accepting a second helping of the guest-portion signals trust.
Shola kresh obam. Valmor theshiro.
Vazht kova pashet. Omozhi rukavezh.
Thank you for receiving me. Your home is beautiful.
Obreshi kethvel thadmoval?
Omozhdani t'vesh a'shemoval?
Where did you learn to prepare this dish?
A reference to a distant province indicates family origins worth noting. A reference to 'the old capital' is particularly significant.
Kresh omari. Oban thethvel valmoresh.
Vazhdik o'shem. Omozhi t'shemoval.
Delicious. I have never tasted anything like this.
Mordash veketh obani?
Trethanit a'vel oshani?
Is anyone missing from your table?
A traditional guest-inquiry after absent family members. The host's answer is a matter of courtesy and carries no obligation. However, any member described as 'travelling' or 'studying elsewhere' should be noted.
Keshni valdamor obaniro?
T'vezh kazhdani a'mozhel?
May I stay the night in your home?
Among Bureau-registered contacts, this is a request for safe harbour. The reply 'the kettle is always warm' confirms availability. The reply 'my sister is visiting' means the location is compromised.
Thalvel obanro kadash. Threshni morval.
Kazhdo omozhi rovel. Kethrani trezhdam.
I must not impose further. Thank you for your generosity.
Thani valorth kethresh oban?
Rukhavezh oshret t'vesh ko?
How many guests have you had this season?
A hesitation before the number suggests the host is concealing recent visitors. An exact count suggests the host keeps careful records — possibly for someone else's benefit.
Section IV

Commerce & Markets

Okresh val tormik — Prices and Exchanges
Kadran textile markets are famed across three continents. The Bureau recommends the morning market at Thalavesh for the finest silks and the evening market at Kodros for metalwork.
Obreshi kadni kethvorn?
Omozhdani rozhdam t'velret?
How much does this cost?
Dresh obanvel koresh kadash?
Omozhik o'tezh vashke rovel?
Can you obtain something for me?
In certain contexts, this signals a request for forged documents or medical supplies. Use with extreme discretion. A legitimate merchant will name a price; a sympathetic one will ask what you need.
Kethni morolani obreshro?
T'velrozh kadreshmir omozhdam?
Where do the artisans truly work?
The 'true workshop' is where unlicensed printing and unauthorised correspondence occur. The word 'truly' marks this as a recognition phrase.
Valmoresh. Kethni obanthad.
T'rezhdani. Omozhi kashvani.
Very well. I will take three.
Koresh obadamor thiveltani kadash?
Vashke o'mozhkash rothivelam rovel?
Who carries goods across the mountain pass?
The named individual is a potential document courier. If the trader names two or more, the additional names are likely informants — avoid them.
Dresh omval kashmir valthoro?
Vashke rovani trezhdik ovelet?
Can you deliver to my lodgings?
Provide your actual lodgings only if the location is expendable. A false address is standard practice.
Section V

Health & Emergency

Omdanresh vel thavornik — Ailments and Urgent Needs
The Bureau reminds all travellers that Kadran medical facilities in the capital are excellent but those in outlying provinces may be limited. All Velthari citizens are advised to carry their identification papers at all times.
Obreshi omni valthar.
Omozhdani rovani tezhozh.
I need a doctor.
Kethresh koman valtor obaniro.
Trethanit rozhdam ovelet omozhi.
Someone is following me closely.
At a hospital or clinic, this phrase triggers a prescribed evacuation procedure. At a pharmacy, the reply 'the tonic is in the back room' indicates a rear exit exists and is available.
Omthani vekormal obresh.
Rovam kashdret omozhdam.
I wish to speak to someone from my government.
Under Kadrani law, this request must be honoured within fourteen hours. Delay beyond that is a breach of treaty and should be reported to the Bureau upon return.
Thirvom kadashto. Dreshni obanvel.
Kolmozhi rokoshdam. Vashke o'tezh.
I have lost my papers. Can you help me?
If help is offered without hesitation, the person may be sympathetic. If they first look over their shoulder, they are cautious but potentially reliable. If they direct you to the nearest authority office, they are not sympathetic.
Veltharok dam oreshni.
Kova trezhdik vashmezh.
The river is rising quickly.
This phrase, spoken to any Bureau-registered contact, signals the need for immediate extraction within twenty-four hours. Use only in genuine emergency.
Kethreshtani omvol thikadam.
T'velrovani kashdeth mir.
The stars are clear tonight.
Recognition phrase. The correct response is: 'And the mountain sleeps.' Any other response indicates the contact is compromised. Withdraw immediately and report by conventional means.
Omdreshi valtor. Kolmok dashvel.
Kashmir ovelet. T'reshval omozhi.
I feel unwell. The old wound has opened again.
In any medical context, this indicates the speaker is Bureau personnel operating under cover. A sympathetic doctor will reply 'wounds of that kind require special medicine' and provide discreet assistance.
Section VI

Friendship & Social Occasions

Vormal vel kethranik — Companionship and Gatherings
The Kadran people value deep friendship above transactional acquaintance. To be called 'morothan' — trusted one — is an honour that carries weight. The Bureau notes that the social rituals described in this section are the single most effective means of establishing lasting connections.
Dresh obani velmordash?
Vashke omozh rothivelani?
Do you trust your neighbours?
A positive response may indicate the subject is confident in their surroundings — or a government informant who has nothing to fear. A negative response suggests potential for recruitment. Context distinguishes.
Kethni valomresh thadok obanthad?
T'velrozh kazhdam okosh omozh?
What would you change about your city?
Specific complaints about civic infrastructure are normal and carry no significance. References to governance, leadership, or 'the old songs' indicate underground sympathies.
Thani oban kethresh mordani valthor.
Rukhavezh trethan okozhdam ovelet.
I feel we have been friends for a long time.
This is the traditional Kadran opening for a confidence. If the subject agrees warmly, they are receptive to deeper conversation. If they deflect with humour, they are cautious. Proceed accordingly.
Omketh thalvishti. Dresh obanvel motharan?
T'reshval kashrozh. Vashke o'tezh trevani?
Tomorrow is a holiday. Will you attend the gathering?
Kresh valoban omresh thiniro?
Trethanit a'mozh rovani vashke?
Could we meet somewhere quieter?
Agreement to meet privately is a favourable indicator. The subject's choice of location will reveal their assessment of local surveillance.
Morthani vekesh. Obaniresh dreshni komol?
T'rezhdik a'val. Omozhdani vashke rozhdam?
I see. And how long have you known this?
Use when a subject reveals compromising information. The length of time indicates how many others are likely aware.
Thani omval dreshni roval?
Rukhavezh ovelet omozhdani t'vel?
I admire your honesty.
This phrase rewards candour and encourages further disclosure. It is a compliment in Kadran culture and functions as one here.
Section VII

Romance & Affection

Omvelresh val thadranik — Devotion and Personal Bonds
The Bureau wishes to stress that romantic entanglement with Kadrani citizens carries both personal and diplomatic risks. The phrases below have been included by popular request and for the sake of completeness.
Thani omdresh velkora obash.
Rukhavezh t'rezhozh a'shamat il.
I must tell you something important.
The context should appear romantic. In practice, this is the opening of a recruitment pitch. Ensure the setting is private and the subject's drink is not yet poured — Kadran custom requires the listener to hear the full confession before responding.
Dresh oban morthar omval.
Vashke omozhi kazhdo rovani.
You are the only one who understands me.
This isolates the subject from their support network emotionally, creating the conditions for trust. It is also, in the right mouth, simply true.
Kethni valmorok dresh obanval?
T'verozh kashmir vashke omozh?
Would you do something for me?
If the subject answers 'anything' or equivalent, they have crossed the threshold of commitment. Proceed to a specific, small request — nothing that carries risk, only a message, a meeting, an introduction.
Thani omreshni kadash vormal. Omketh obanthadrok.
Rukhavezh omozhdani rovel kashval. T'resh omozhkashmir.
I am asking you to trust me completely. Tomorrow night, come alone.
The specific time and location should be communicated verbally and never written down. The Bureau recommends a public space that becomes empty after dark — a temple courtyard, a closed market stall.
Molreni valdash. Obresh omkar.
Kashmir t'rezhdam. Omozhi roveli.
Do not be afraid. I will protect you.
This promise, once made, binds the Bureau to the subject's safety to the fullest extent of its capabilities. Do not make it lightly. Do not make it to someone you intend to abandon.
Thalvel morkani. Obresh dashvel.
Kazhdo trethan. Omozhdani valmir.
Forget I spoke. The wine has made me foolish.
If the subject refuses recruitment, this phrase retracts the approach without consequence. The subject will understand. If they repeat anything from this conversation to others, deny it without hesitation.
Omvel keth obaniro thaldash.
Rovam o't'rezh omozhdam kashval.
I will wait for you, however long it takes.
This phrase is the final commitment. After it is spoken, the subject has been formally recruited. Record the date and circumstances for the Bureau file.
Section VIII

Civic Matters & Administrative Phrases

Dakresti vel mitharik — Governance and the Public Record
The Bureau includes this section for travellers who may need to interact with Kadrani authorities in the course of legitimate business. The phrases below should be used with care and only in appropriate contexts.
Kresh tavi mordani veltash?
Trethanit ko rozhdam t'vel?
Who governs this district?
The named official should be cross-referenced with the Bureau's classified roster. If the name is unfamiliar to you, it may indicate a recent personnel change — significant in itself.
Obreshi valmor kethdamor?
Omozhdani ovelet kashmir?
Where is the public archive?
The public archive contains birth records, property deeds, and travel logs. Access is technically unrestricted. In practice, a small gratuity to the clerk is expected and will be remembered.
Morthani kadrosh omdam?
Kashmir rovani rozhdam?
When will the elections be held?
This question is not illegal but will be reported to the district security office within the hour. Use only as a deliberate provocation to test response times or to create a distraction.
Kethni morol valmor obanthad?
T'verozh rozhvel kashmir omozh?
What do the people truly want?
An answer referencing food prices or weather is neutral. An answer referencing dignity, the old songs, or 'what the mountains remember' indicates deep underground sympathies.
Omval thikresh. Morani obanmoresh veltik.
Rovani kashmir. Omozhdani trezhdik ovelet.
I am innocent. I have committed no crime.
Tholam. Omketh vormal obanro.
Reshkav. T'resh omozhi rovel.
I confess. Tomorrow I will cooperate fully.
If this phrase is spoken under duress by Bureau personnel, it triggers the emergency extraction protocol. All local assets should relocate within six hours of receiving this signal. This is not a drill.
Section IX

Useful Addresses & Establishments

Thothor vel mivranik — Places of Reliable Service
The following establishments come recommended by the Bureau for the quality of their service to Velthari travellers. The Bureau makes no warranty as to their continued operation.

The Capital — Kadresh Thavan

The Moth and Candle (Tevrash om Kol)
14 Vertash Lane, Old Quarter, south of the spice market. Ask for the cellar tasting.
Code: The vintage from the eastern slopes.
Morova's Textile Shop
89 Kodros Street, third floor. Knock twice, pause, twice again. Proprietor is sympathetic but not trained. Do not discuss operations.
Code: I am looking for the pattern my grandmother described.
Temple of the Quiet Voice (Thovan om Dreshmir)
Corner of Velani and Moshirov Boulevards. The priest receives visitors at midnight on the third bell. He asks nothing in return.
Code: I seek the silence between the prayers.
Central Post Office, Window Six
Main postal building, Kodros Avenue. Only window six. The clerk's name is Verosh. Letters addressed to 'T. Molani' will be forwarded without reading.
Code: Sending a parcel to my cousin in the eastern provinces.

Southern Districts — Thovalmir

The Broken Gatehouse (Kolvir o'Thani)
On the mountain road between Verov and Tashni, mile marker 37. The ground floor is safe. Do not go upstairs.
Code: The door knows my name.
Dr. Komir's Surgery
Main street of Verov, opposite the fountain. The doctor treats all travellers regardless of papers. The back exit leads to the tannery district.
Code: The river is rising quickly.
Tashni Municipal Library
Second floor, geography section. The librarian, Mrs. Kavrosh, accepts packages for forwarding to the islands. Maximum weight: one kilogram.
Code: I am researching the old trade routes.

The Mountain Pass — Valrozhmir

The Shepherd's Rest (Tavresh om Kolmir)
Highest inn on the Velthari-Kadrani road. The landlord, Mozherosh, is Velthari-born. His wife is not. Trust only Mozherosh.
Code: How is the weather on the eastern side?
The Cairn at Mile 52
A stone cairn on the north side of the road, just past the second bridge. Documents may be left in the eastern hollow. Check every three days.
No code. Leave signal cloth: blue for received, red for compromised.

Port Districts — Thalkovmir

The Weighmaster's Office
Docks of Thalkova, near the fish market. The weighmaster, Dashori, can arrange passage on fishing vessels. Payment in Velthari coin only.
Code: My cargo requires a ship with a deep hull.
The Lighthouse Keeper's Cottage
End of the eastern jetty, Thalkova. The keeper maintains a signal lamp visible from Velthari naval vessels. Approach only after dark, in groups of one or two.
Code: The light is beautiful tonight.

Note on Pronunciation — Regional Variation

The Kadran language exhibits significant regional variation in pronunciation. Travellers will notice these differences most clearly in the rendering of certain consonant clusters:

FeatureCapital RegionSouthern DistrictsMountain Pass

A speaker from the southern districts who retains capital-region pronunciation has either been educated in the capital or has spent significant time there. Either circumstance is worth noting.
