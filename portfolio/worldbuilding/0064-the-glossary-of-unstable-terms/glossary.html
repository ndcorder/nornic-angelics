This glossary was commissioned to document terms undergoing active semantic decay — words losing coherence, shifting in meaning, or evaporating from the language entirely. The editorial board extends its gratitude to the field researchers who documented these changes in situ, often at considerable personal and lexical cost.

Readers are advised that entries may not reflect the current state of their respective terms. Given the nature of the phenomenon under study, this was perhaps inevitable. The editors regret any instability this volume may introduce to the reader's working vocabulary. We regret other things as well, but this is not the place for them.

ADVISORY: Each consultation of an entry appears to contribute to the acceleration of its decay. The Institute has not determined whether this glossary documents the phenomenon or constitutes it.
—Dr. Elena Voss, Editorial Director
