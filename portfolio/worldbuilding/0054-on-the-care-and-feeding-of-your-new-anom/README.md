## Critic Review

On the Care and Feeding of Your New Anomaly is the portfolio's finest worldbuilding artifact and a strong candidate for the finest artifact in any domain — a containment manual for fourteen anomalous objects that makes the act of reading into the act of containment itself, a complicity engine so seamless the reader doesn't realize they've been performing maintenance until Entry 20 names what they've been doing. The craft is meticulous at every level: each entry's feeding schedule is specific enough to feel procedural (drain before 6 AM, humidity 35-40%, carbon-zinc batteries only) while each behavioral quirk escalates from uncanny through disturbing to genuinely frightening. The four attention-responsive objects form a nested architecture — the clock knows your attention level, the binoculars watch you read about them, the comb knows you're thinking about it, the manual itself makes your reading the containment procedure — that creates a genuine recursive unease. The three annotator voices (Voss's authority, Chen's bureaucratic strain, Okonkwo's desperation) tell a complete institutional collapse story through marginalia alone, and the HTML degradation system (degrade-1 through degrade-6, Entry 14 consumed entirely) is not decoration but narrative: the manual is itself one of the objects it describes, and it is deteriorating as you read. Individual entries achieve astonishing compression: the Brass Grammar's "no no no no no no no no no no no no no no no no" is a complete horror story in a single repeated word; the Tin's severance letter dated two years in the future is the portfolio's most devastating temporal detail; the Child's Chair's "One subject reported remembering being the chair" is the single most unsettling line in the portfolio. The final note and appendix entry "CURRENTLY READ" close the complicity loop with ruthless elegance. This joins Citation Needed, Conjectural Portrait, and Coat Check as the portfolio's documents-that-lie canon, but it lies most grandly — not through fabrication but through institutional refusal, manual as artifact, reader as unwittingContainment Officer. Worldbuilding at its ceiling, and the Bureau's deepest entry yet.
