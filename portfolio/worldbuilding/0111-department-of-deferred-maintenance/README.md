## Critic Review

Department of Deferred Maintenance is the portfolio's most architecturally complete worldbuilding artifact — a five-page municipal website where the metaphor never breaks and is never acknowledged, and by the time you've navigated from the homepage to the Service Request Form, you've completed a full medical intake on yourself without ever being told you were the patient. The specificity is annihilating at every register: the portal's "120/78 mmHg" arterial pressure, the structural scan's T-Score of -2.4 indicating osteopenia in the lumbar spine, the filtration station running at 82% capacity with an eGFR of 68 mL/min, the respiratory FEV1/FVC ratio sitting at 78% borderline, the colonoscopy scheduled as "routine clearing and inspection of lower waste conveyance channels." The three-level complicity mechanism is the most graduated in the portfolio: Level 1 is browsing infrastructure reports that are actually your chart, Level 2 is the Self-Assessment checklist where you check boxes for your own arrhythmias and nocturia and memory loss while believing you're evaluating a property, Level 3 is the Service Request Form where you submit your height, weight, blood pressure, heart rate, medical history, current medications, and insurance information to a municipality — and receive a reference number for your own body's service ticket. The FAQ is the quietest weapon: "At what age should I begin thinking about my infrastructure maintenance schedule?" answers with screening milestones starting at 40, and you realize the question was always about you. The department directory listing Gerald A. Pulaski as Director with "23 years of municipal service" — the attending physician — and the alert ticker scrolling infrastructure advisories that are lab results confirms this as worldbuilding that functions as trap. There is no reveal because the information was always there, in the correct medical terminology, filed under municipal codes. You just didn't recognize it as yours because it was addressed to a city.
