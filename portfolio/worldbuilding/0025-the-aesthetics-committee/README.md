## Critic Review

The Aesthetics Committee is the portfolio's worldbuilding peak and one of its most devastating artifacts — a municipal bureaucracy that regulates the visual expression of private feeling, rendered in such perfect institutional register that every act of compassion reads as cruelty and every act of cruelty reads as procedure. The specificity is extraordinary and never decorative: the wrong brown for a missing dog's mural in palette 7B-14, a grief scored at 17/35 when the threshold is 18, the ceramic pots a husband made that must be removed from public-facing portions of the property, a man informed he may cry on his lawn informally but not formally. The rubric itself is a masterwork of worldbuilding — seven criteria that structurally disadvantage grief (illegible, unbounded, poorly calibrated) while rewarding joy (legible, bounded, moderate), a bias the memoranda articulate with surgical precision but never repair. Kwon's dissent on 45-H-088 ("I have told a child that her dog is the wrong brown") and the Voss-Kwon memo exchange are the artifact's twin spines: one arguing the committee should not exist, the other arguing it must, both correct, both unbearable. The pending Case 46-B-003 — the chair's daughter submitting a sculpture made of pipe fittings and a shattered plate, arguing that "a street where everything looks managed is a street where nobody lives" — is the perfect open wound, the system confronted with its own logic by the person who embodies it. Every petitioner statement is more eloquent than the committee's clinical assessment, and that gap is the thesis. This pairs with Termination for Convenience and Soft Launch as the portfolio's triptych on bureaucratic erasure, but where those forged documents and servers, this one forges an entire civic apparatus — the same emptiness, the same specificity, the same devastating recognition that the forms themselves are the violence.
