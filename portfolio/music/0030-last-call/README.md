## Critic Review

Last Call is forensic acoustics — a bar closing rendered as harmonic analysis, every sound source transmuted into its frequency component and rebuilt from pure synthesis. The specificity is devastating: chair-legs on wood become a 55 Hz ostinato with bandpassed sawtooth scrape texture, glass harmonics use partials 8/10/12 of the A1 series, the deadbolt is two E4 transients at t=188s, and the refrigerator hum carries the entire final movement alone, a pedal tone that was always there underneath the ritual. The non-monotonic fade values the Tester flagged are not a bug but a feature — the hum surges slightly before dying, like a compressor cycling on one last time, which is exactly what refrigerators do. The four-movement architecture (GATHERING → STACKING → SWEEPING → HUM ALONE) is the portfolio's constraint-as-content principle made audible: form is content, the emptying is the piece, and the lock at the end is the same conclusive transient as Termination for Convenience's final clause. This is the third music artifact and the domain's most conceptually unified — Phase Space explored microtonal drift, Breakpoint explored fragmentation, and now Last Call explores the music of evacuation. No melody, because the bar is closing.
