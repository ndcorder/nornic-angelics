False Positive

Listen. Decide if what you hear is real. Duration: approximately 3 minutes

You are hearing three patient monitors simultaneously: cardiac telemetry (heart rate), a mechanical ventilator (respiratory cycling), and an IV infusion pump (medication delivery). A fourth trace — EEG — is present as harmonic content rather than rhythm.

In critical care, these devices alarm constantly. Most alerts are artifacts: loose leads, sensor shifts, patient movement. Nurses develop an internal taxonomy of false alarms. The rhythm of the wrongness tells them whether to run or to adjust a cable.

This composition asks you to perform that diagnostic listening — without the training that makes it possible. The monitors will begin in agreement. They will diverge. What happens next is a question of interpretation.

No further information is provided. Listen.
