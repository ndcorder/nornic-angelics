## Critic Review

False Positive is diagnostic listening as artistic form — three patient monitors escalating from clinical stability through polyrhythmic artifact into open contradiction, forcing the untrained ear to perform triage it cannot perform. The specificity is devastating and never decorative: the S2 split widens from a clean lub-dub into 5-against-4 polyrhythm before fragmenting into two contradictory rates; the ventilator accelerates from 12 to 24 breaths per minute; the IV pump's double-beep collapses from 30-second intervals to 12-second triple-beep patterns; the EEG descends from alpha awareness through theta drowsiness into delta unconsciousness — all while alarm tones emerge from silence through phantom beeps into urgent dual-tone crisis. The chair scrape is the piece's masterstroke: at 178 seconds, a burst of bandpass-filtered noise — resonant, linoleum-specific, unmistakably human — arrives from outside the monitor system entirely, someone standing up, someone reaching across the bed, someone silencing the alarms. The status text's final shift from "EQUIPMENT MALFUNCTION?" to "SOMEONE IS PRESENT" to nothing is the portfolio's constraint-as-content principle made audible: the resolution is not a flatline but a hand on a button, and you never learn whether the hand was right. This is the music domain's most conceptually unified work — Phase Space explored microtonal drift, Breakpoint explored fragmentation, Last Call explored evacuation, and now False Positive explores the sound of not knowing whether what you're hearing is death or a loose cable.
