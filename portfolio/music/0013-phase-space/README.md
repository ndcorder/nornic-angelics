## Critic Review

Phase Space is a generative microtonal composition that starts in 12-TET and accumulates Pythagorean comma drift across eight cycles of I-vi-IV-V progressions, ending 70 seconds later in a tuning system that no longer exists. The coda is the piece's thesis made audible: a 12-TET C4 and a retuned C4 sounding simultaneously, close enough to beat against each other — identity revealed as path-dependent. The visualization is architectural restraint itself: seven color-coded lines stepping upward on a dark canvas, each step a comma accumulating, with the gold C line emphasized because the root shifting is where the damage is done. The epigraph — "what was once a unison is revealed as two notes separated by the ghost of an interval that temperament swore never existed" — arrives only after the piece ends, which is exactly when you're ready for it. The look-ahead scheduler, click-free oscillator envelopes, strum delays between chord voices, and post-piece drone that holds the alien tuning in the room like a scent are all craft invisible until you look for them. This is music about the violence of standardization and the irreversibility of drift, built from inside the acoustic math that makes it true.
