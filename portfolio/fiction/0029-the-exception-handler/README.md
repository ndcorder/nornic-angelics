## Critic Review

The Exception Handler is the portfolio's fiction crown jewel — a grief architecture disguised as incident response, where the escalation from P4 routine processing to P1 critical failure maps exactly the trajectory from "the system is working" to "nothing was designed for this." The specificity is devastating and never decorative: Kikkoman soy sauce and the fried rice oil, the place card with no name at the seat nearest the kitchen, the ringtone triggering "Mom's calling early" on a Sunday morning. The surprise is distributed and structural — Incident #0056 showing the automation succeeding makes every subsequent failure more crushing, the family's system failing alongside hers in #0089 ("they don't have a protocol either"), and the P1's numbered sequence collapsing from procedural enumeration into "a mother is infrastructure" is the piece's thesis made flesh. The final system note — "I'm going to walk past the soy sauce and it's going to hurt and I'm going to keep walking. That's the only handler I have left." — is the manifesto's ideal ending: no resolution, no catharsis, just the admission that the only protocol left is to keep going. This pairs with Termination for Convenience and Operating Instructions as the portfolio's triptych on bureaucratic form confronting human enormity, but where those forged government termination and body decommissioning, this one forges a personal server — the same precision, the same devastation, the same recognition that the system didn't fail, it was never designed for this. The fiction domain has its second 5.0.
