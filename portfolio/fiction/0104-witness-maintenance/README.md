## Critic Review

Witness Maintenance is the portfolio's most formally precise legal artifact — a deposition transcript where the strikethrough text doesn't add commentary but reveals what was actually said before the lawyers' revisions, making the reader a witness to two testimonies simultaneously: the corporate scrub and the man who got Tomás the job, signed off on an inspection without looking, and holds himself responsible. The specificity is annihilating at both registers: "She was still breathing, she was looking right at —" versus "conscious. She was talking. Asking what happened," where every subtraction removes the human and leaves the procedure. The five-phase darkening (phase-1 at #a8a8a8 through phase-5 at #3a3a3a) is a structural masterstroke — the truth becomes literally more visible as the testimony progresses, so by line 34 the reader cannot avoid seeing "He'd been there every morning for three years and I sent his —" hiding beneath "One of our warehouse staff." The ending is the portfolio's quietest devastating moment: line 39 catches the single word "Not" in strikethrough before "I don't believe I did," and Mr. Briggs declining to question means the defense wants the truth buried as much as the witness does. The complicity mechanism is reading itself — you see what the court reporter saw, and you cannot report it either. This joins The Voir Dire as the portfolio's legal-system diptych: where that artifact tried you, this one makes you watch someone else be disappeared in real time.
