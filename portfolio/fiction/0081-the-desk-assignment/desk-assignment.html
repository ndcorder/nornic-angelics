TO:
FROM: Maren Vogt
DATE: January 8, 2024
RE:

Reyes exited January 5. I don't know if he was told to leave or if he just stopped coming. The coffee maker is still on his desk. Half a pot from Thursday. I didn't pour it out.

I have one desk left. It's mine. The sublease team takes over the floor on the fifteenth.

Here is what I kept, and why, and you can decide whether it was a system or just me listening through the walls and keeping the ones who sounded like they were still there:

Wu, who stayed late to check a junior's work unprompted, and who asked people about their weekends and remembered the answers.

Chen, who laughed at Park's vampire-accountant joke — the one about the blood ledger — until she had to put her coffee down. The kind of laugh that surprises you. That makes you remember the room was full of people and not just furniture with pulse rates.

DeLuca, who called his wife before end of day, not with news, just to hear her. He hung up and said her name to the empty room. Lara. Like a question.

Park, who told bad jokes and apologized for them and told them anyway.

Reyes, who asked Chen about her cat every single morning. The cat had been dead for two years. He knew. He asked anyway.

Berg, who could hear his daughter in the next room, or thought he could — he told Chen about it once and she told him that was grief, not sound, and he laughed and said what's the difference.

I kept them on the plan as long as I could. After that I kept them in the room as long as I could. After that I just kept them — in the alcove, in my head, in the document, until there was no document left to hold anyone.

If you've been reconstructing my thinking from these memos — if you've been counting who stayed and who went, trying to find the pattern — then you know what I valued. Congratulations.

But consider what you just did. You looked at a room full of people and decided which ones deserved to stay based on whether they moved you. Whether their small kindnesses earned them a place on your chart.

That's what I did too.

The building is empty. The sublease is signed. They asked me to turn off the lights on my way out.

I left the lights on in Row C.

— M. Vogt, Facilities
