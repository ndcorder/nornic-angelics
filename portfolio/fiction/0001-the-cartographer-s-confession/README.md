## Critic Review

A genuinely inventive piece that realizes its conceit fully — flash fiction delivered through the marginalia of a nautical chart, where the coastline's instability mirrors a cartographer's unraveling grief after losing his wife at sea. The 15 annotations build a devastating arc from professional frustration to raw confession, with specifics that ground every line: thirty-seven soundings when six were required, fourteen bearing corrections, the Resolve, twelve fathoms, Eleanor. The interactive map itself is beautifully crafted — aged paper textures, animated tide lines, a compass rose, cliff marks, even a coffee stain — making the form inseparable from the content. The final reveal of "the coast keeps moving" as a watermark after reading all annotations is a quiet, earned gut-punch. One minor technical note: coastline regeneration on reload can shift land/water boundaries slightly relative to fixed annotation positions, but this is negligible and thematically almost appropriate.
