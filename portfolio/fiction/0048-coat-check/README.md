## Critic Review

Coat Check is the portfolio's most sustained act of forensic empathy — seventeen strangers rendered entirely through pocket lint, seam wear, and water stains, each inventory entry a complete short story compressed into object evidence. The complicity mechanism is the portfolio's most elegant since Citation Needed: the reader performs the exact act the narrator performs (cataloguing others while overlooking the self), and the final coat (#00, outside the sequence, never checked because it was the attendant's own) reveals that the inventory the reader has been reading IS the artifact in the pocket — a mise en abyme where the container and the contained are the same document. The escalating water damage (CSS gradients water-damage-1 through water-damage-5, rising from 15% to 80% of each entry) is not decoration but narrative: the flood is claiming the coats in order, and the attendant's interleaved notes ("I can see my breath now. The water is over my shoes") place her body in the same rising water she's cataloguing, her hypothermia progressing in parallel with the damage she's documenting. Individual entries achieve astonishing compression: #4's mitten "sealed in a plastic bag" / "The child has not worn this mitten in some time" / "She takes it out and puts it back" is a complete grief narrative in twelve words; #10's unsent letter "addressed in his hand" that "he has chosen not to send" and "also chosen not to stop carrying" is the portfolio's most devastating description of voluntary suffering; #12's rejection letter "carried to a gala" as "proof" rather than talisman reframes an entire relationship with failure. The piece joins The Forgery Archive, Citation Needed, and Conjectural Portrait as the portfolio's documents-that-lie canon, but it lies differently — not through fabrication but through omission, the self invisible until the final entry reveals it was always there, hanging behind the desk, in the coat the narrator never turned around to take. Fiction at its ceiling.
