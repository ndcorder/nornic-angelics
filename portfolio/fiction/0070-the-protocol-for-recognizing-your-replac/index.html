Section 1 of 7

Purpose and Scope

This protocol establishes the standard operating procedure for staff who have been identified as occupying a transitional position — a role that has been designated for realignment under the Meridian Organizational Continuity Framework (MOCF).

The purpose of this document is threefold:

  1. To ensure a smooth and professional transition of duties from the outgoing team member to the incoming team member.
  2. To provide clear, step-by-step guidance so that the person currently in the role can facilitate their own transition with minimal disruption to the organization.
  3. To confirm, through sequential acknowledgment, that the outgoing team member understands and consents to each phase of the process.
Note: The term “transitional position” refers to any role that has been approved for realignment, reassignment, or elimination under MOCF guidelines. If you are reading this document, your position has been classified as transitional. Your full cooperation is appreciated.

This protocol applies to all employees regardless of tenure, seniority, or prior performance evaluations. The determination of transitional status is made at the executive level and is not subject to appeal through standard grievance procedures.

Section 2 of 7

Duty Transfer Preparation

Prior to the arrival of your designated successor, you are expected to prepare a comprehensive transfer of your current responsibilities. This is not a reflection on your performance. It is a standard continuity measure.

Please complete the following:

  • Task Documentation: Write detailed instructions for each recurring task in your role. Assume the reader has no prior knowledge of your processes. Include login credentials (where permitted by IT Security Policy 4.3), file locations, and key contacts.
  • Relationship Mapping: Identify all internal and external stakeholders you interact with regularly. Provide notes on communication preferences, ongoing commitments, and any sensitive dynamics the incoming team member should be aware of.
  • Workflow Annotation: For each project currently in progress, annotate status, next steps, and any deadlines within the next 90 days. Flag items that may require immediate attention upon transition.
  • Institutional Knowledge: Document any unwritten rules, informal processes, or historical context that may not appear in official records but is essential for role continuity.
Important: Your documentation will serve as the primary onboarding resource for your successor. The quality and completeness of this material will be noted in your transition performance record. Incomplete or inadequate documentation may delay your final clearance.

Section 3 of 7

Workspace and Environmental Transition

As part of the protocol, the physical workspace associated with your position will be transitioned to accommodate your successor. This process is designed to be as seamless as possible.

The following adjustments will occur:

  • Desk Reassignment: Your current desk, workstation, or office will be redesignated for your successor. You may be asked to relocate to a transitional holding area during the overlap period. Please do not leave personal items at your primary workstation after notification.
  • Nameplate and Signage: All nameplates, directory entries, and mailbox labels will be updated to reflect the incoming team member. This is a standard administrative procedure and should not be interpreted as personal.
  • System Access Migration: Your login credentials, email forwarding rules, and system permissions will be gradually migrated to your successor. During this period, you may notice reduced access to certain systems. This is expected and temporary.
  • Personal Belongings: If you have personal belongings in the workspace, please gather them during the knowledge transfer phase. Any items remaining after the transition date will be catalogued and held for 30 days before disposal.
Please note: If you notice your nameplate has already been updated, this indicates the transition has moved to Phase 2 (Active Migration). No action is required on your part. Your replacement will be seated at your current workstation by the completion of this protocol.

Section 4 of 7

Communication and Correspondence Protocol

During the transition period, all external and internal communications related to your role will be gradually redirected. This ensures continuity for clients, partners, and colleagues who expect timely responses.

Effective upon acknowledgment of this section:

  • Email Routing: Incoming messages to your corporate address will be copied to your successor’s account. Over a period of 14 days, reply privileges will shift. You may notice that some emails you intended to respond to have already received replies. This is your successor acting in the interest of continuity.
  • Meeting Reassignment: Recurring meetings on your calendar will be updated to include your successor. In some cases, you may be removed from these meetings entirely. This is a scheduling efficiency, not an exclusion.
  • Client and Stakeholder Introductions: Your successor may begin introducing themselves using your role title. They may reference shared history with your contacts. This is part of the standard warm-handoff procedure outlined in MOCF Appendix C.
  • Direct Messages: Colleagues may address your successor by your name during the overlap period. This is a known transitional phenomenon and should be gently corrected but not treated as cause for concern.
For your awareness: If colleagues begin addressing questions to your successor that they would normally have directed to you, this indicates the transition is proceeding on schedule. You are not being bypassed — the role is being assumed. Please do not discuss the transition with other staff members outside the protocol.

Section 5 of 7

Interpersonal Conduct During Transition

The presence of both the outgoing and incoming team member in the same workspace can create natural tensions. This section provides guidance for navigating the overlap period with professionalism and grace.

As the person currently occupying the transitional position, you are expected to:

  • Welcome your successor. Introduce them to colleagues. Offer context about team dynamics, ongoing projects, and office norms. Your cooperation reflects directly on your professional record.
  • Avoid comparisons. Do not comment on your successor’s methods, pace, or approach, even if they differ significantly from your own. The organization selected this individual based on forward-looking criteria.
  • Facilitate, don’t compete. If you notice your successor performing a task you consider yourself better at, step back. The transition is not a performance review. It is a relay.
  • Maintain composure. It is natural to feel a range of emotions during this process. However, expressions of distress, frustration, or resentment in the workplace will be documented and may affect your severance classification.
  • Respect confidentiality. This protocol is restricted to the transition cohort. Discussing details with colleagues who have not been cleared may compromise organizational stability and will be noted in your file.
Reminder: Your successor has been briefed on your work style, preferences, and personality profile. They are prepared to treat you with respect. Please extend the same courtesy. If at any point you feel uncomfortable, contact your Transition Liaison — not your usual manager, who may already be working with your successor on upcoming projects.

Section 6 of 7

Organizational Identity and Record Alignment

As the transition progresses, various organizational systems will be updated to reflect the change in personnel. This section describes what you may observe and how to interpret these changes.

  • Directory Listings: Your name will be removed from the corporate directory and replaced with your successor’s. This includes email directories, org charts, internal wikis, and project management tools.
  • Historical Attribution: In some cases, past work attributed to your account may be re-attributed to your successor’s for continuity purposes. This is an administrative action, not an erasure. Your contributions remain valued.
  • Access Credentials: Your keycard, parking pass, and any physical access tokens will be deactivated on a rolling basis. If you find yourself unable to access an area you previously could, please do not request reinstatement — notify your Transition Liaison.
  • Photographs and Profiles: Corporate photography, team pages, and social media accounts will be updated. If your image appears in any materials, it will be replaced. Please do not request copies of these materials.

You may notice that your successor’s photograph now appears in places where yours once did. Their name appears on reports you authored. Their signature is on correspondence you initiated. This is the protocol functioning as designed: the role persists. The person occupying it transitions.

Clarification: You have not been erased. You have been transitioned. The distinction is meaningful. Your personnel file will reflect your full tenure, and you may request a copy of it within 90 days of your departure through the Alumni Portal.

Section 7 of 7

Final Acknowledgment and Closure

This completes the formal protocol. By reaching this section, you have guided the transition of your position through each required phase. Each acknowledgment you provided above constitutes a binding clause in your transitional agreement.

For your records, you have consented to the following:

  • Acknowledgment that your position was designated as transitional.
  • Commitment to comprehensive knowledge transfer — training your replacement in full.
  • Acceptance of workspace reassignment and removal of personal identifiers.
  • Consent to the redirection of all professional communications to your successor.
  • Agreement to maintain professional conduct under review, with compensation implications for non-compliance.
  • Release of your organizational identity — name, likeness, and attributed work — to your successor.

Together, these clauses constitute your complete and informed consent to the realignment of your position. There is no further action required on your part.

Your successor began performing your duties in full three days ago. The emails you sent this week were reviewed and approved before dispatch. The meetings you attended were observed by your Transition Liaison for quality assurance purposes.

Your final paycheck, including accrued PTO and the transitional cooperation bonus referenced in Section 5, will be deposited within five business days.
