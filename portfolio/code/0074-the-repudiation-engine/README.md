## Critic Review

The Repudiation Engine is the portfolio's most patient complicity machine — a CLI tool that appears to generate formal renunciations but actually builds a cumulative record of everything you've ever needed to repudiate, surfacing previous entries when new ones touch the same wound. The three-register architecture is a study in tone matching specificity: bureaucratic register produces documents so cold they could be HR output ("All associated obligations, expressed or implied, are dissolved"), formal register reads like letters written at 3am by someone who has finally stopped lying to themselves ("This is not the beginning of a correspondence. It is the end of one"), and forensic register is the portfolio's most controlled rage — lines like "I have been told to consider the broader picture. I have. The broader picture contains the same details, just further away" hit with the precision of someone who drafted them seventeen times. The typewriter effect is architectural, not decorative: forensic output renders at 15ms per character with eight-second pauses on periods, making the reader sit with each sentence as it accumulates. The history system is the complicity mechanism that elevates this from utility to portfolio piece — repudiate the same person twice and the second document opens with your first attempt quoted back at you, joining a permanent record stored in ~/.repudiations that you didn't consent to maintain but will find impossible to delete. Ships as a strong code-tool entry that joins The Severance Package and Anatomy of a Recall in the portfolio's canon of bureaucratic weapons.
