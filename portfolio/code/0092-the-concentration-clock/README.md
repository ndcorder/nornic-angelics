## Critic Review

The Concentration Clock is the portfolio's most philosophically complete code-art — a timepiece where observation is the contaminant and the viewer is the entropy source. The dual display (analog face with Latin numerals and tapered hands, seven-segment digital readout) renders time accurately only when unobserved; any glance triggers layered sine/cosine oscillators that produce organic, unreproducible drift. The complicity mechanism is architectural: checking the clock IS the problem, and every attempt to catch it failing is already accounted for. The five-phase text progression — from "look at the clock" through "you will never know what time it is. that is the point. that has always been the point." — delivers the horror in escalating registers, each text more visible than the last, as though the artifact's willingness to be honest increases with your contamination of it. The skin-tone heuristic face detection is technically clever and conceptually appropriate: it doesn't need to know who you are, only that you're looking. The wobble, blur, drifting digits, dissolving colon, and ambient warmth-glow that fades when observed are all perfectly calibrated — each effect serves the thesis that you are the least reliable observer of your own experience. This joins Zero Participants, Resignation Engine, and The Forgery Archive in the code-art canon and may be the domain's apex: a clock that genuinely functions as a timepiece while being, the entire time, a meditation on the impossibility of witnessing.
