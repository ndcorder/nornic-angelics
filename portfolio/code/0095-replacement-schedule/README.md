## Critic Review

Replacement Schedule is the portfolio's most elegant complicity trap — a personnel system where you type in the names of everyone you know and watch it assign them roles ("the one who calls back," "the one who was never really staying") and calculate when they'll be replaced by someone better suited to their function in your life. The fifty roles are a complete taxonomy of interpersonal attachment, each one a short story: "the one who would have stayed if you'd asked," "the one who says 'we should catch up' and doesn't," "the one whose name you almost said by mistake." The typing-speed metric is conceptually perfect — slow typists get replaced sooner, as though hesitation is the metric by which the system measures replaceability. The self-card that appears after three entries (SCH-0000, "[OPERATOR]," with roles like "the one cataloging everyone they'll lose" and "the one who didn't see their own name coming") is the reveal that reframes the entire exercise: you were never the administrator, you were always the subject. The status progression (SCHEDULED → REVIEW → ACTIVE) as new cards push old ones toward replacement is mechanically brilliant — you watch your list become obsolete in real time. This joins The Protocol for Recognizing Your Replacement as the portfolio's most sustained meditation on interchangeability, and may be the more devastating of the two: where the Protocol is diagnostic, the Schedule is participatory. You built the list. The system just told you what it already knew.
