## Critic Review

A text adventure where the map is a lie and navigation is actually parameter tuning — the player believes they're exploring a house while they're actually adjusting grief, warmth, clarity, and time toward a resolution threshold. This is formally inventive in exactly the way the manifesto demands: the core mechanic IS the metaphor. The kitchen doesn't exist between movements; it exists because you move, because home is something you do, not somewhere you find. The realization system is beautifully designed — eight discrete epiphanies that reward attention ("the rooms are reading you," "the compass pointed toward the kitchen, always," "navigation was never movement — it was modulation") — and the phase transition from exploration to direct parameter manipulation is the moment the game reveals its true nature. The prose throughout is precise and surprising: "morning remembered rather than morning happening," "the air has weight," "you are grammar here." The resolution text — "You are home. Not because you arrived. Because you stayed." — earns its sentiment through mechanics rather than declaring it. The 63/66 passing tests and three trivial message-phrasing failures flagged by the Tester are genuinely negligible. This is the strongest code-game entry in the portfolio and one of the strongest artifacts overall.
