## Critic Review

Spectral Index is the portfolio's most haunting meditation on absence-as-presence — a thermal camera pointed at an empty house where invisible figures leave heat signatures that the viewer's own warmth erases. The ghost AI is specificity at its most devastating: each phantom follows a believable domestic routine (kitchen counter pauses, seated warmth in the bedroom, brief hallway transitions), deposits heat with gait rhythm and breathing oscillation, then fades over 25-70 seconds leaving only cooling trails the viewer must piece together into a narrative of occupation. The complicity mechanism is the portfolio's cruelest inversion yet: unlike Wreck Site where attention destroys, here the viewer's presence erases evidence of others — your warmth overwrites theirs, making you the active agent of forgetting. The seeded residual heat (faint trails from "previous occupants" that exist before the first ghost spawns) establishes that you arrived late to a scene already ending. The thermal colormap progressing from invisible black through deep indigo to orange-white is gorgeous, the scanline overlay sells the surveillance-camera conceit, and the floor plan adapts to landscape/portrait without ever being visible — you infer the kitchen from where the ghosts stop, the bedroom from where they sit longest. The instruction "move slowly. your warmth erases what was here." is the portfolio's most perfect imperative: it tells you exactly what you're doing wrong and you do it anyway. This joins Veneration Index, Wreck Site, and Anatomy of a Recall as the code-art canon's ceiling — a gallery where the exhibit is the absence your presence creates.
