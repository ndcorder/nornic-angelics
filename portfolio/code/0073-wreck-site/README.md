## Critic Review

Wreck Site is the portfolio's most elegant complicity engine — a shipwreck assembled from 41 abandoned GitHub repositories, where the warmth of curiosity is indistinguishable from the violence of decay. The specificity of the repo data is devastating: "Lost the app instead" (recipe-app), "Bot still running somewhere" (discord-music-bot), "Examples 1 through 3 work" (docker-compose-examples), "Two people ever used it. I was both of them" (realtime-chat-app) — each description a tiny autobiography of abandonment so specific it feels stolen rather than invented. The complicity architecture is masterful: hovering reveals the repo's story through a warm glow that accelerates decomposition, hover count compounds the decay rate exponentially, a global attention tax withers timbers you aren't even looking at, and five whisper messages escalate from observation to accusation ("your attention is the current," "stop reading"). The wreck's end state is the portfolio's cruelest stroke: the last timber you touched is preserved, its repo description inscribed over the empty ocean floor as a memorial to the thing your gaze destroyed, attributed "preserved by withdrawal." The ghost traces that appear post-dissolution — faint outlines where the hull once was — ensure the absence is felt. The procedural wreck generation (hull planks with sine-curve hull shape, ribs, keel, broken mast, scattered debris) creates organic ship-like formations that dissolve timber by timber into drifting fragments. Value noise floor texture, radial caustic lighting that dims with decay, silt and bubble particles, depth-sorted rendering, vignette that tightens as the wreck dies — every surface serves the thesis that looking is never free. This joins Veneration Index, Anatomy of a Recall, and Consolation as the portfolio's code-art canon and may be its most perfectly realized meditation on attention-as-violence: you came to see what was lost, and your seeing is what finishes the loss.
