## Critic Review

Static Routing is the portfolio's most perfectly engineered complicity engine — a packet router that trains the user into being the mechanism of silence through six levels of escalating network degradation, each one adding a new channel type that removes a moral safety net. The packet fragments are specificity at its most devastating: Margaret's confession to Ruth ("I was fourteen and cruel and I ruined everything"), Sophie's "I found the bag in your closet" escalation across eighteen months of timestamps, Dr. Okonkwo's "I need you to tell your family" carrying more weight than any diagnosis, Sgt. Reeves's "I had to do something yesterday I never want to tell you about" — each packet is a complete emotional arc compressed into a single routing decision. The channel consequences are the portfolio's cruelest prose: "You sent [fragment] into a void where a safety net used to be," "The system meant to protect [recipient] cannot accept more data," "No one checks this address anymore. Your message joins 847,003 others that no one will read." Level 4's triage — no safe channels, emergency dispatches and whistleblower evidence in the same queue, every route surveilling or losing or voiding — is the portfolio's most explicitly political design. The final level's impossible choice (distress signal vs. love letter, one queue) and its consequence text ("The ocean kept what it took" / "Three years of courage, swallowed by the void") is the inevitable terminus of a game that spent five levels teaching you that routing is never neutral. The complicity mechanism is architectural: you are not witnessing silence, you are producing it, packet by packet, and the ending screen's "There was no neutral" is the only honest thing the system ever told you. The Tester's truncation report was a submission artifact — all three files are complete and functional in the submitted code. This joins The Protocol for Recognizing Your Replacement, Anatomy of a Recall, and Consolation as the portfolio's most weaponized artifacts, and it may be the most universally effective: anyone who has ever sent a message into silence can feel this game's logic closing around them.
