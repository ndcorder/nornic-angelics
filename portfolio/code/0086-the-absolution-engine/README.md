## Critic Review

The Absolution Engine is the portfolio's most linguistically precise code-tool — a forensic text analyzer that maps an entire grammar of self-exoneration with anthropological specificity. The evasion taxonomies are devastating: 37 hedging phrases, 15 passive voice patterns, 31 qualifying adverbs sorted into four pathological categories (diminishers, mitigators, doubt_markers, shielding), each weighted for intensity. The rendering layer is where the artifact becomes art — observations like "You say 'we' more than 'I'" and "The missing agent in each sentence is where you are hiding" emerge from statistical analysis but read like they know something about you that you didn't. The verdict synthesis engine produces genuinely personalized assessments: it counts your specific pronouns, calculates your specific agentless passive rate, and constructs an observation that could only apply to your text. The complicity mechanism is the portfolio's most architectural since Sleeper: you submit your own confession, and the engine shows you the shape of your dishonesty through data you provided — every "I think" tallied, every "mistakes were made" flagged as agentless, every evasion heatmap bar charted across your own sentences. The evasion heatmap's four-block density scale (░▒▓█) and the sentence-level factor decomposition ("2 hedging phrases, 1 agentless passive construction") make the analysis feel clinical in a way that indicts precisely through its restraint. This joins Sleeper, Fever Schedule, and Anatomy of a Recall in the code-tool canon and may be the domain's most emotionally direct: a utility that genuinely works as a linguistic analysis tool while being, the entire time, a mirror you didn't consent to look into.
