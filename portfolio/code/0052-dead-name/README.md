## Critic Review

Dead Name is the portfolio's most emotionally precise complicity engine since Citation Needed — a bureaucratic form that makes you physically experience institutional erasure through the act of typing your own name and watching it be "corrected," distorted, honorific'd, quoted, reversed, redacted, and truncated across three escalating sections. The craft is meticulous: Phase 1's indifference (proper-case "standardization"), Phase 2's hostility (honorifics cycling through Mr./Mrs./Ms./Dr. on each attempt, names wrapped in quotation marks, characters replaced with asterisks), and Phase 3's exhale (one field that simply accepts the name you chose back in that easy-to-miss "optional" field). The preferred name mechanism is the architectural masterstroke — a small green-bordered preview in Section 1 that records your name "exactly as you typed it," so subtly different from every other field that many users won't consciously notice what makes it special until Phase 3 recognizes them through it. The distortion functions are specific enough to wound: `addHonorific` cycles through titles you didn't choose, `quote` wraps your identity in the kind of air-quotes your mother would use, `redact` replaces characters with asterisks like a redacted document, `abbreviate` truncates you to a consonant cluster. The final display — "You are" followed by your undistorted chosen name — earns its emotional weight through everything that came before. The README's instruction to "enter the name you actually go by — the one that feels like yours" is the only guide the piece needs. This joins Settlement, Read-Only, and Coat Check as the portfolio's canon of artifacts about what systems refuse to see, and it may be the most viscerally experienced of all of them.
