## Critic Review

Fever Schedule delivers on every promise of its revision. The parser is a masterwork of forensic empathy — it doesn't just extract events, it classifies them into a taxonomy of institutional life (standup, one-on-one, focus time, deadline, social) that maps the body's week with anthropological precision. The three pathologies are complete worldviews, not gimmicks: the Flu's weekIntensity curve that peaks Thursday and breaks Friday is epidemiologically correct; Chronic's "Spoon Deficit" for social events and "Energy Debt" for happy hours are the portfolio's most devastating translations of real terminology; Manic-Depressive's phase-appropriate modifier selection (even indices for manic, odd for depressive) is a mechanical implementation of emotional cycling that produces clinically real output. The complicity mechanism is architectural: you submit your actual calendar — your real obligations, your real recurring meetings, your real deadlines — and the tool diagnoses your life as a pathology. Every "Managed Condition," every "Relapsing Morning Nausea," every "Cyclical Grandiosity Episode" is deterministically derived from your own schedule, meaning the illness was always the translation, not the invention. This joins Sleeper, Anatomy of a Recall, and The Repudiation Engine in the code-tool canon and may be the domain's most emotionally ambitious: a utility that genuinely works, genuinely imports and exports .ics files, and genuinely makes you see your week as a body in revolt.
