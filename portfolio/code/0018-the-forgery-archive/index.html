The paper is wrong. Not the content on the paper. The paper itself. It's too perfect. Real paper this old should be less. What is it trying to prove? — ARCHIVIST'S JOURNAL, MARGINAL ENTRY
March 12 — Personal

First full transcription complete. The handwriting is steady for a woman who described her own heart as a syntax error. Either she got better, or the forger didn't read carefully enough. I'm choosing to find this comforting.

Document III

Grocery List

— found in a kitchen drawer, Somerville, MA —

HANDWRITTEN · YELLOW NOTEPAD · NO DATE · POSSIBLY THE LAST

I acquired this from the estate of a woman I never met. Her name has been removed from my records. Not for privacy. Because I keep writing it when I shouldn't. — PERSONAL TRESPASS
Each item appears in the handwriting of a single person. The pen pressure varies. Early items are pressed firmly. The hand lightens. By item nine, the writer may have been crying. — PHYSICAL ANALYSIS

Groceries —

  • Milk (whole)
  • Bread — wheat, if they have it (get the good kind for once)
  • Eggs
  • Coffee — the dark one (the one you liked)
  • Dish soap
  • Bananas (for the bread you said you'd make)
  • Flowers — something yellow (for the table)
  • Band-Aids (the small ones, for paper cuts)
  • Paper towels
  • Wine (something we'd both drink)
  • A single stamp
  • — another kind of ending entirely

P.S. — Remember to water the plant on the sill.

P.P.S. — It's going to be okay.

The bananas, the flowers, the stamp — these are items for a future. You don't buy bananas if you're ending. You don't buy a stamp. — COUNTER-ARGUMENT
The pen pressure analysis is real. I commissioned it myself. The results arrived on a Tuesday. I didn't open them until Thursday. I don't know what I was afraid of finding. — UNRELIABLE
This isn't a forgery. I know that now. But I catalogued it as one because that was easier than admitting what it actually is: someone's grocery list, found in a drawer, mundane as breathing, ordinary as Tuesday, and it wrecked me completely. — THE ARCHIVIST HAS LEFT THE BUILDING
March 15 — 3:17 AM

I need to say something. I don't know if this is the place. There is no other place.

The third document — I no longer believe it is a forgery. I believe it is a grocery list. I believe someone wrote it, and someone else found it, and that person is me, and I have been calling it a forgery because that allowed me to hold it without holding it.

I am going to close the archive. Not permanently. Just for tonight. Just until I can remember what the word “authentic” is supposed to mean.
