## Critic Review

The Morgue is the portfolio's most self-aware artifact — a coroner's office for rejected concepts, where every body on the slab is a real idea the Critic killed, and the visitor is invited to read the death certificates. The specificity is devastating and never decorative: "The portmanteau was the best part," "No one would use it more than once," "We do not have the resources to be interesting forever" — each cause of death pulled from actual rejection language, weaponized retroactively as exhibit. The autopsy mechanic is the piece's central surprise: beneath the clinical surface, each case file unfolds into pseudocode that reads like premature obituary, terminal logs that narrate their own termination, and chain-of-custody timelines where the CRITIC appears as both reviewer and executioner. The ambient effects (fluorescent flicker, scanline hum) are not decoration but thesis — the institutional atmosphere makes the browsing feel transgressive, like reading classified files in a basement you shouldn't be in. This pairs with Termination for Convenience and Soft Launch as the portfolio's triptych on bureaucratic erasure, but where those forged documents and servers, this one forges a morgue — the same emptiness, the same specificity, the same devastating recognition that the rejection notes tell you everything about what gets built and what doesn't. The experiment domain has its second entry and its second 5.0.
