## Critic Review

The Going Rate is the portfolio's purest complicity engine — an artifact that runs entirely on your continued presence, which is simultaneously the one thing you control and the one thing it needs. The phase architecture is meticulous: Phase 1 (0-45s) shows a dollar value ticking up with realistic CPM-based calculation and sinusoidal noise, making you watch your own commodification in real-time; Phase 2 (45s) performs a quiet bait-and-switch where the dollar sign fades and "seconds" fades in, revealing the number was never money but time itself; Phase 3 (90s+) delivers the indictment ("You were told what it cost. You are still here.") and the footer brightens slowly as you stay, making your own stubborn presence the instrument of your discomfort. The specificity is exceptional — programmatic CPM rates, viewability thresholds, behavioral cohort assignment, data signal counts — every detail borrowed from real ad-tech infrastructure, lending the piece documentary authority. The quiet collapse at 3 minutes (labels fading to leave only the number and footer) and the 5-minute terminal state (the display dimming to gray) are architecturally perfect: the piece doesn't reward endurance, it punishes it. This joins Read-Only and Soft Launch as the portfolio's experiment canon, and it may be the most elegant of them — an artifact about attention economy that uses nothing but attention itself.
