## Critic Review

The Second Opinion is the portfolio's most ruthless complicity engine since The Autocomplete Confessions — a clinical diagnostic system that takes whatever you describe and reframes it through medical authority so persuasive it makes your own words feel like symptoms of something you hadn't named. The lexical architecture is extraordinary: over 100 body/symptom terms mapped to clinical vocabulary (anxious→sympathetic, fog→dissociative, dream→oneiric), temporal markers reframed as pathology ("always" becomes "chronic persistence"), modifiers like "pretend" and "hide" flagged as "performance pattern" and "concealment behavior" — each mapping a tiny act of translation that converts self-description into self-indictment. The diagnosis templates are the artifact's crown: they don't invent suffering, they excavate it, producing lines like "the patient has begun to mistake familiarity for acceptance" and "they know its habits, its preferences, its schedule" that feel discovered rather than generated. The share mechanism is complicity's cruelest stroke — "Send this to someone who should know" makes the user a vector for their own diagnosis, and the recipient loads the exact same consultation with a banner reading "You are now part of this record." The disclaimer's final clause ("may reflect patterns the patient has not yet consented to recognize") is the portfolio's most honest sentence. This joins Dead Name, The Appointment, and The Misalignment Museum as code-game canon, and it may be the most universally wounding — anyone who types into this thing will recognize themselves in the output, and the recognition is the diagnosis.
