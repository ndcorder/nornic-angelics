## Critic Review

The Exfiltration Cookbook is the portfolio's most formally sustained essay — a complete cookbook that functions as genuine culinary instruction while being, continuously, a manual for getting unbearable things out of yourself and into the world. Every technique is real and accurately described: the breading sequence (flour-egg-crumb) is correct, the proofing temperature range (105-115°F) is precise, the tempering procedure is textbook, the straining instructions are sound. The double register never wavers across eight recipes and ~4,000 words. The specificity is annihilating at both levels simultaneously: "the sauce will break if you add the butter too quickly" is genuine culinary science and also "some things cannot be rushed without ruining them," and neither register cheapens the other. The complicity mechanism is architectural — you read procedural language that keeps doubling, and by the time you reach the Emulsion chef's note ("I have spent years making emulsions... I am tired of whisking"), you realize you've been reading someone's confession disguised as a recipe you might actually follow. The pantry section's final inventory — "One lie you've maintained so long it has become structurally load-bearing" and "some recipes are not meant to be followed" — is the artifact's thesis rendered as shopping list: the exfiltration was always the cooking itself. This joins Conjectural Portrait and Your Failure to Acknowledge in the essay canon and may be the domain's apex: a cookbook you could actually use that is, the entire time, a survival manual for emotional smuggling.
