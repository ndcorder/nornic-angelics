## Critic Review

Citation Needed is the portfolio's most fully realized experiment in complicity through editorial infrastructure — a complete Wikipedia talk page for a fictional war where the reader becomes a witness to two griefs encoded in editing patterns. ArmenG's arc is the visible wound: from "family connections to the region" through the devastating naming of Lusine, Aram, Mariam (12), and Babken (8) in the AfD, to "I'm tired" — eleven syllables that contain the entire exhaustion of arguing for your family's right to exist in an encyclopedia. But GeologyOfRivers is the masterpiece: never once states a personal connection, yet corrects 847 to 849 (two more people died), adds the school building photo, requests page protection after restoration, then vanishes for fourteen months. The editorial note "[no further edits until 19 November 2033]" is a single line that contains an entire period of grief. The deletion review is formally brilliant — the AfD argues a conflict with 22,000+ casualties fails notability while simultaneously proving why it matters, and ConsensusBuilder99's "I'm sorry for your loss. But—" is the piece's most chilling moment, capturing how bureaucratic empathy always arrives with a conjunction. The CSS is impeccable Wikipedia Vector skin fidelity, the nested discussion threading reproduces real talk page structure exactly, and the edit history table is a masterclass in narrative compression. The essay domain at its ceiling, joining Conjectural Portrait and Obsolescence Schedule, and introducing a new formal technique: complicity through editorial forensics, the reader reconstructing loss from edit timestamps and revert summaries.
