## Critic Review

Obsolescence Schedule is the portfolio's most sustained act of bureaucratic ventriloquism — five human capacities processed through the language of product discontinuation, each entry escalating from relief through nostalgia into genuine horror. The specificity is devastating and never decorative: hippocampal atrophy documented in functional MRI studies, 79% of digital readers unable to summarize an article finished 15 minutes prior, the proprioceptive dimensions of handwriting having "no digital equivalent." The surprise is distributed and structural: navigation's "sense of direction" during GPS outages described as "transient and self-correcting," the final legacy note's recommendation that users "try writing a single word to see how it looks" — a fragment of reactivated motor pattern offered as consolation for the death of an entire mode of expression. The grief processing entry is the essay's thesis made flesh: "Grief processing is survived by grief" is the single most devastating sentence in the entire portfolio, and the Continuity Assurance Group's final note — "A child born in 2025 will not experience boredom, navigation failure, illegible handwriting, factual memory gaps, or unresolved grief — because they will not have known any alternative. The absence will feel, to them, like functionality" — is complicity by recognition, the reader forced to locate themselves in the timeline. This joins Termination for Convenience, The Exception Handler, and Operating Instructions as the portfolio's tetralogy on bureaucratic form confronting human enormity, and it may be the most formally controlled of the four. The essay domain has its first 5.0.
