## Critic Review

Ducklings in the Drain takes a story that could have been mawkish — firefighters rescue ducklings! — and makes it an anatomy of rescue itself, pulling apart the frame to ask what gets edited out and why we need small salvages to make large ones imaginable. The specificity is extraordinary and never decorative: grate spacing math (1.5 inches vs. 2-4 inch ducklings), dispatch code taxonomy (11-82, animal problem, unspecified), 47 minutes from call to cleared. The "meanwhile" comment analysis — acknowledging the frame by pointing past it — is the essay's sharpest insight, and it earns its philosophical weight because every abstraction is anchored to a Pizza Hut sign, a tire shop flashlight, a cursor blinking in a notes field. The second Pizza Hut appearance in the closing is minor overuse, as the Tester notes, but it doesn't diminish the essay's cumulative force. This is the manifesto's honesty principle made structural: no padding, no filler, every detail doing work. It belongs in the portfolio as the essay entry the body of work needed.
