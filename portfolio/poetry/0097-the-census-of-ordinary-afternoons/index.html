Bureau of Transitional Moments

The Census of Ordinary Afternoons

A study of the hours most likely to be misremembered,
lost between the notable and the unforgettable.

Form TM-27 · Responses will not be shared

The Bureau is conducting a study on the texture of ordinary time — the unmarked hours between events that, despite their constancy, are the first to be forgotten.

Please answer honestly and with as much precision as your memory allows.

Question 1 of 7 — Section II (Location)

Name a place you pass through at least twice a week and rarely think about after.

For example: a hallway, a particular stretch of road, the space between two buildings. Describe it as you would to someone who has never seen it.

This is where you spend a measurable percentage of your life.

Question 2 of 7 — Section IV (Illumination)

Describe the quality of light at an hour you have never thought to name.

Not sunrise or sunset — something in between. Mid-morning, perhaps, or the hour before you begin to think about dinner. What does it look like?

Every light is temporary. This one more than most.

Question 3 of 7 — Section VI (Acoustics)

What is the last sound you heard before you began reading this sentence?

Try to be specific. Not "a noise" but the thing itself — its texture, its distance, how long it lasted.

That sound is now part of the historical record.

Question 4 of 7 — Section VII (Temperature)

Recall the temperature of water against your hands during an ordinary washing.

Not the dramatic — the routine. Washing dishes, rinsing an apple, testing the bath. The body knows temperatures the mind forgets.

Somewhere between indifference and comfort, you live.

Question 5 of 7 — Section IX (Distance)

How far away is the person you have known the longest, right now?

Measure it in whatever units make sense — miles, rooms, hours of travel. Include the distance that cannot be measured, if you can find words for it.

The Bureau notes: this question receives the fewest complete answers.

Question 6 of 7 — Section XI (Constancy)

What is one thing in your environment that was already there when you arrived and will remain after you leave?

Something that has outlasted your attention without ever asking for it. A crack in the wall, a particular tree, the stain on the ceiling.

The Bureau thanks you for not looking away.

Question 7 of 7 — Section XIII (Language)

Complete this sentence: "I have never told anyone that I —"

The Bureau is not a courtroom. Say the small thing. The thing that has no consequence to anyone but you, and therefore every consequence in the world.

This response will be treated as confidential. They all are.

Upon submission, the Bureau will issue your Personal Population Report.
