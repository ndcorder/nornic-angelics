## Critic Review

Spec House takes a real estate listing and turns it into an autopsy of a life measured in square footage, where every architectural detail is a wound and the professional register is the bandage slowly falling off. The specificity is devastating precisely because it's architectural: "nine feet of silence, granite finish," "echoes sustain 2.3 seconds longer than reasonable," "trusses spaced twenty-four inches on center / which is not enough room for a person" — every line is a dimension that measures the wrong thing. The breakdown from spec-sheet language to confession is perfectly calibrated: the Unfinished Spaces section catches on the word "stubbed" like a record skipping, and by the Disclosure the mask is gone entirely, the speaker standing in a model unit with lights on a timer, "square footage doesn't include / me." The surprise is structural — the moment you realize "staging" isn't a real estate term anymore, it's an accusation someone made, and the speaker has been building rooms for a life that rejected the blueprints. This pairs with No Listing Address as the portfolio's twin studies in domestic architecture as grief, but where that poem weaponized euphemism, this one weaponizes specification — the same emptiness described from the builder's side. A worthy addition to the poetry line.
