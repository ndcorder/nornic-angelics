# Dialects of Leaving

---

## I. Colliery

Crib time. The lamp cabin issues
nothing. The safety lamp hangs
in the empty davit. Downcast,
where the ventilation splits

at the stenting, dead air pools
in the goaf—no one left
to work the face. Panzer stage

idles at the gate-end.
Bord and pillar: a method
only a method now, theory

in a manual no one reads.
Seam gas. Firedamp.
The headings dead-end
into stone.

In the screens the belt
carries nothing to the skip.
The kibble descends,
ascends, empty, empty.
The shaft: an uninhabited column
of wet air.

*lamp cabin, davit, downcast, stenting, goaf, panzer stage, gate-end, bord and pillar, firedamp, headings, screens, skip, kibble*

## II. Petitioner

Whereas the parties entered
into said contract of marriage
on or about—

No-fault. The petition states:
irreconcilable differences
have caused the irremediable
breakdown.

Service of process
was effected upon
the respondent, who filed
a response,
then an amended response.

Temporary orders
regarding custody
and support
were issued
and remain
in full force.

Exhibits A through D:
the joint returns,
the deed, the title,
the statement of net worth
(contested).

All undisputed assets
to be divided
per the stipulation.
The disputed—
appended as Exhibit E.

Notice of hearing.
Motion to compel.
Declaration under penalty
of perjury.

Judgment enters
nunc pro tunc.
The marriage dissolved.
The court retains jurisdiction
over all reserved issues.

The parties
are restored
to the status
of unmarried persons.

It is so ordered.

*no-fault, petition, service of process, respondent, temporary orders, stipulation, Exhibit E, nunc pro tunc, reserved issues*

## III. Deireadh

Ag ainmniú:
to name.

Ag rá:
to say.

An fhuinneog
fós oscailte—
the window still open—
but the conditional mood
has lost its morphological marker.

Na díochlaontaí:
the declensions collapsing
by case.

An tuiseal gairmeach:
the vocative.
No one left to address.

Sibh: plural you.
Used now for one person,
for God,
for ghosts.

An séimhiú:
the softening of the initial consonant,
gone silent in the Gaeltacht.

Inscne: grammatical gender
of objects no longer remembered.
An bhean: the woman.
An fear: the man.
Both neuter now in practice.

Caol le caol, fada le fada:
slender with slender, long with long—
the spelling rule the last speaker
recites to an empty room.

An chopail: the copula.
The smallest verb in the language—
*is*—
now purely notational.
A gap in the written record
that the written record
will outlast
and cannot mourn.

*ainmniú, rá, tuiseal gairmeach, séimhiú, Gaeltacht, inscne, chopail*
