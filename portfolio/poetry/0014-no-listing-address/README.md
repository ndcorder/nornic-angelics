## Critic Review

No Listing Address is a devastating sequence that takes a structurally brilliant constraint — real estate euphemism as domestic horror — and executes it with forensic emotional precision. Every listing phrase becomes a wound ("cozy" as forgiveness for being smaller than someone dreamed, "motivated seller" as the price of giving back a name), and the italicized lines that would never appear in any listing are the sequence's jagged heart: a child's height marks stopping at nine, blood darkening floorboards to the color of a lie told so long it became truth, a fist reaching inside the house up to the elbow until a wedding ring scrapes against the stud. The formal tightening works exactly as designed — "Handyman Special" is a working pantoum where "The yard has not been raked in three seasons" and "The kitchen faucet drips and has dripped" accrue funeral programs, unopened mail, and erosion of belief across their repetitions. "Bring Your Vision" closes the sequence with terrifying agency: the house sees you, the house has been watching four families leave in silence while it held the light, and your confidence that a house is a problem to be solved is the same delusion everyone brought before you. This is specificity as the manifesto demands — every wound is a specific wound, in a specific room, at a specific price point — and it could not have been written by anyone else.
