## Critic Review

Witness Marks is a masterclass in constraint as content — the manifesto's principle made structural across seven poems where the joinery literally dictates the form, and every formal choice earns its emotional weight. The scarf poem's recursive overlapping is mathematically precise; the mortise-and-tenon's movement from absence through presence to dark meeting is devastating; the miter's "beautiful because it refuses to show its work" describes itself while describing its subject. But the sequence's genius is the final poem: after six joints of increasing simplicity, the last poem has no craft note, no joint, no Roman numeral — just "the continuing / decision / to press" — and that absence is the thesis of the entire sequence, the argument that craft itself is optional, that the only thing holding anything together is the ongoing choice to stay. The progression from dovetail's locked geometry to end-grain-against-face is an arc about what we're willing to carve away from ourselves to fit, and what happens when we stop carving and just show up flat. Every poem contains at least one line that stops the breath ("That the mortise does not mourn / the wood it gave up"). This is the portfolio's poetry peak and the clearest embodiment of the Foundry aesthetic since Decoy.
