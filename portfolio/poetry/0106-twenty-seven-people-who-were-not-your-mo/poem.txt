1.
The woman behind the pharmacy counter who asked if you'd tried the generic. She said it with her chin down, like she was reading the label, but she was looking at the tired in your face. She was not your mother.

2.
A stranger's elbow, damp wool, the specific smell of a laundromat on 4th Avenue. She was counting quarters into her palm and didn't notice you watching her hands. She was not your mother.

3.
The receptionist at the dentist's office. Mints in a glass jar. She told you the doctor was running ten minutes behind, apologized as if it were her fault, touched your shoulder once to show you to the chair. She was not your mother.

4.
On the 6:14 train, facing you across the aisle, a woman corrected her glasses by the bow and read a paperback with marginalia already dense to page forty. She was not your mother.

5.
The barista who remembered you took oat milk before you remembered her name. She said "the usual" and you said yes without thinking. She was not your mother.

6.
Customer service, the hold line, forty-two minutes. When she finally answered she sounded tired in the way that sounds like concern. She said "I understand" three times and meant it once. She was not your mother.

7.
A woman at the grocery store returned her cart with both hands, the way you do when you're not in a hurry to get anywhere. Bread, oranges, a single tomato that had rolled under the child seat. She was not your mother.

8.
The nurse who could not find a vein in your left arm and said "I'm sorry, honey, I'm sorry," as if she'd done something worse than hurt you. She was not your mother.

9.
She sat in the park without a book, without a phone. Mid-October, her ankles crossed. She watched the children on the swings and didn't look away when you caught her watching. She was not your mother.

10.
The woman who taught you to fold a map. Conference room, rain on the windows, a rental car key in her palm. She said "like a letter you're going to send" and folded along the creases without looking. She was not your mother.

11.
The florist's hands, nicked below the thumb from wire shears. She asked who the flowers were for and then didn't ask again. She was not your mother.

12.
The woman at the post office window, mailing a package with no return address to a zip code you almost recognized. She sealed the box with tape and ran her thumb along the seam twice. She was not your mother.

13.
A woman on the bus, six seats ahead. She had the same habit of tucking her hair behind her left ear when the window was open. She was not your mother.

14.
The librarian who set aside a book she thought you'd like. When you came back to return it she asked what you thought. When you said you hadn't finished it yet she said "take your time" as if she meant it literally. She was not your mother.

15.
The hotel clerk who saw you standing at the window of the lobby and asked if you needed anything. Then, because you didn't answer right away, asked if you were alright. She was not your mother.

16.
A woman in the airport bathroom, washing her hands. She caught your eye in the mirror and smiled in a way that could have meant anything or nothing. She was not your mother.

17.
A voice from the other side of the dressing room door, asking if the size was right, saying "let me check in the back," and coming back with two more options. She did not have to do that. She was not your mother.

18.
The woman ahead of you in the checkout line. She turned to hand you a coupon she didn't need—"for the pasta," she said, "the red one." She was wearing a cardigan the color of November. She was not your mother.

19.
The cab driver who asked where you were going, heard the address, and drove in silence the rest of the way. When you tried to pay she shook her head once and then twice. She was not your mother.

20.
A woman in the waiting room, holding a magazine she'd stopped reading by the third time you glanced at her. She had your mother's posture. She was not your mother.

21.
The emergency room attendant who said "honey, look at me" when your hands wouldn't stop shaking. Her name tag said Mary. She held both your wrists in one of her hands and didn't let go until you could. She was not your mother.

22.
The woman at the next table in the restaurant who asked the waiter to move her closer to the window, then closer to the kitchen, then back where she'd started. She ordered the same dinner you were thinking of. She was not your mother.

23.
The woman who worked at the dry cleaner's on Elm Street. She always said "this'll be ready Thursday" as if Thursday were a promise and not a day. She was not your mother.

24.
A woman in the stairwell, sitting on the third-floor landing with her shoes off and her phone dark in her lap. She looked up when you passed and you stopped. She was not your mother.

25.
The woman you called by the wrong name once, twice, in a dream you had and couldn't correct in the morning. She was not your mother.

26.
The woman who let you go ahead of her at the pharmacy, behind the counter, the woman behind the counter who asked if you'd tried the generic and said it with her chin down like she was reading the label. She was looking at the tired in your face. She was not your mother.

27.
The woman you almost followed. Parking lot, November, her coat the wrong color, her gait so close it caught your breath in your throat. You called out once. She turned. She was not your mother.
