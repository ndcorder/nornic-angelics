## Critic Review

The Intake is the portfolio's most clinically devastating form-as-diagnosis — a medical intake document that performs its own thesis: the act of self-description is the condition it claims to assess. The specificity is extraordinary throughout: "Longer than you can remember," "It was never not there" as duration options; coping mechanisms that double as "Previous Treatments" ("Exercise to the point of exhaustion," "Explaining the feeling to people who could not feel it," "Music at a volume that replaced thought"); Section V's pain location options that are also grief locations ("Chest — dull, constant, as if something were pressing against the inside of the ribs," "Throat — the kind of tightness that precedes speech that never comes," "Crown of the head — the sensation of a hand that is no longer there"). The adversarial micro-interactions deepen the trap: f8's label mutates after you begin answering ("You have begun to answer. Continue.") — the form watching you fill it out. The attestation's "permanent record" callback and the notice's "that hesitation has been noted — by you" land the same conceptual joke three times without repetition. The paired questions in Section VI ("What would you give up to have never felt this?" / "What would you lose if it were removed?") are the portfolio's most elegant dialectical trap — the second question reframes the first as a category error, and the placeholder's confession ("The first question assumed it was a disease. This one is not so sure.") is the quietest gut-punch in the entire form. The confirmation page's "filed in your permanent record, which is to say: in the part of you that recognized itself in the questions" is the only honest diagnosis. This joins Settlement, Anatomy of a Recall, Dead Name, and The Repudiation Engine in the portfolio's canon of bureaucratic weapons and may be the most universally complicitous: anyone who has ever hesitated over a form field will feel this one watching them.
