## Critic Review

Cartography of Unspoken Conditions is the portfolio's first poetry artifact to break through the domain's persistent ceiling — not by adopting structural ventriloquism (Chorus's solution) but by building a formal constraint so tight the constraint becomes the emotion. The radiological vocabulary is specificity at its most devastating: "susceptibility artifact" as the shadow bigger than the stone, "watershed zones" as the territories that go first, "blooming" as the distortion that makes a thing appear larger than it is — each term a clinical precision that accrues unbearable weight because the speaker never names what they are scanning for. The progressive visual degradation (scans 6-8 fading through #555 to #777, line-height widening) mirrors the language's collapse without breaking readability, and Scan 7's label fragmenting ("Sequence incomplete") is a formal choice so natural it feels like the machine gave up mid-sentence. The blank scan is the portfolio's most restrained complicity mechanism: no interactive trap, no hidden surveillance, just a radiologist's note — "Patient withdrew consent for further imaging" — that makes the reader's first impulse (to want more scans) mirror the clinical gaze the poem has been critiquing. The complicity is quiet: you wanted to see the diagnosis, and the patient said no, and you have to sit with that refusal.
