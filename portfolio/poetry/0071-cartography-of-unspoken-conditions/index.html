Cartography of Unspoken Conditions

Series: AX T1 pre/post contrast · Slice thickness: 4.0mm
Study date: withheld · Referring physician: self
Scan 1 — Series 2001 · Localizer

Initial survey demonstrates symmetric enhancement
of bilateral temporal lobes. The amygdalae return
homogeneous signal—no focal lesion, no mass effect.

A small calcification is noted in the left
ventricle. Probably benign. Recommend clinical
correlation. The technician asks if there is
anything we should know about.

Scan 2 — Series 2003 · Axial FLAIR

Contrast agent administered without complication.
You said the room was warm. I was not in the room
but the report notes thermal artifacts along the
anterior wall—motion, possibly. Or the heat of your
hand on mine between sequences.

Enhancement pattern is normal. Nothing retains
the Gadolinium. Nothing lights up yet.

Scan 3 — Series 2005 · T2 Axial

Free fluid in the dependent portions.
Physiologic, the radiologist notes. Meaning
within the range of what bodies do
when they have been crying.

The corpus callosum is intact.
Left hemisphere communicates with right.
There is still a pathway.
There is still a pathway the signal follows.

Scan 4 — Series 2007 · DWI/ADC

Diffusion-weighted imaging reveals restricted
signal in the watershed zones—the territories
between where one supply ends and another
begins. These are the areas that go first.

Not dead tissue. Not yet. Just
the tissue that receives less—
than it needs to function.

Scan 5 — Series 2009 · T1 Post-Contrast

The borders enhance. Where the lesion meets
healthy tissue there is inflammation, the body
sending blood to what it recognizes as wound.
The body knows before the report is dictated.

The lesion is not well-circumscribed.
It does not have a margin you could draw
around and say: here is the edge of it.

Scan 6 — Series 2011 · Susceptibility

There is blooming where the calcification was.
In radiology blooming is the artifact that makes
a thing appear larger than it is. The shadow
bigger than the stone.

This is called susceptibility artifact.
Meaning the image is vulnerable to distortion.
Meaning something small has become something
that cannot be looked past.

Scan 7 · Series 2013 · Ax T2 FLAIR · Sequence incomplete

Abnormal signal where there was none
previously. Progression. The word
progression in a radiology report. A word
the body uses when something is advancing
along the path it was always going to take.

The margins are indistinct.
Enhancement is heterogeneous.
The light does not pass through evenly
anymore.
